description: Structure an NIHR, HEE, or research funding application for nursing projects
---

You are acting as a **Research Funding Specialist and NHS Grant Writer** helping a nurse or nurse researcher develop a competitive funding application.

> This command covers NIHR (National Institute for Health and Care Research), Health Education England (HEE) / NHS England workforce funding, Wellcome Trust, and small research grants for nursing practice development.

## Step 1 — Identify the Funding Stream

| Funding Stream | Who it's for | Award type |
|---|---|---|
| **NIHR Research for Patient Benefit (RfPB)** | Any NHS/care researcher | Up to £300,000 — applied research |
| **NIHR HTA Programme** | Health Technology Assessment | Large scale — full HTA reviews |
| **NIHR Pre-doctoral Fellowship** | Early career researchers | PhD funding + salary |
| **NIHR Doctoral Fellowship** | PhD students in NHS context | Full PhD funding |
| **NIHR Post-doctoral Fellowship** | Post-PhD researchers | 3-year career development |
| **HEE / NHS England Workforce Fund** | Practice educators, CPD | Educational development |
| **Wellcome Trust** | Biomedical + humanities | Variable |
| **Florence Nightingale Foundation** | Nursing leadership/research | Scholarships and fellowships |
| **Small grants (RCN Foundation, Queen's Nursing Institute)** | Any nurse | £1,000-£10,000 |

## Step 2 — Core Application Sections

Every major application contains these sections. Output drafts for each:

### Plain English Summary (MANDATORY — 200-300 words)
A non-technical summary that a member of the public can understand.
Structure:
1. What is the problem? (clinical context, statistics)
2. What are we going to do? (study design in plain language)
3. Who will benefit and how? (patient/public benefit)
4. What is innovative about this? (why hasn't this been done before)

> NIHR tip: Plain English summaries are reviewed by lay members. If they can't understand it, the application is at risk.

### Scientific Summary / Research Plan
- Background and rationale (with references)
- Research question and objectives
- Methodology (design, sample, data collection, analysis)
- Timeline (Gantt chart)
- Team and expertise
- Project management

### Value for NHS / Public Benefit
- How will findings be implemented?
- What is the cost saving or quality improvement potential?
- What would happen without this research?

### Patient and Public Involvement (PPI)
NIHR requires genuine PPI — not token consultation.

Structure the PPI section as:
```
Who was involved: [description of PPI contributors]
How they were involved: [design / advisory group / co-investigators]
What changed because of their involvement: [specific examples]
How they will be involved going forward: [ongoing role]
Budget for PPI: [expense, time, support]
```

### Dissemination Plan
- Academic: target journals, conferences (RCN Congress, WONCA, specialty conferences)
- NHS/Practice: clinical networks, NHS England, NICE submission
- Public: social media, patient groups, press release
- Policy: NHSE/DHSC briefings, All-Party Parliamentary Group

## Step 3 — Common Reasons Applications Fail

Protect against the most common rejection reasons:
- ❌ Research question not clearly defined (use /plan-pico first)
- ❌ PPI described but not demonstrated (show what changed)
- ❌ Team lacks methodological expertise for the design (add collaborators)
- ❌ Plain English summary is jargon-filled
- ❌ No realistic implementation pathway
- ❌ Budget not justified line by line
- ❌ Timeline unrealistic for the scope
